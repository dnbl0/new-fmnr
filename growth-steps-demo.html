How it works

Four steps to restore degraded land

No planting. No nurseries. FMNR works by protecting and managing trees that are already trying to grow from existing root systems hidden beneath the soil.

Step 01

Identify

Locate surviving root systems in degraded fields. These “underground forests” are already present on most degraded land.

Step 02

Select

Choose the strongest, most useful stems and protect them from being cut or grazed. Remove competing shoots to direct energy upward.

Step 03

Prune

Regularly prune side branches to produce tall, strong trees. Pruned material feeds soil, livestock and households — nothing is wasted.

Step 04

Protect

Establish community rules to protect regenerating trees. Within 3–5 years, degraded land transforms into a productive agroforestry system.
