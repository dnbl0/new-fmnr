What partnership includes

Everything you need to make it work.

Training, technical support, measurement tools and a global network — all included in every partnership.

Training & Capacity

Comprehensive programs for field staff, extension workers and community facilitators — in-country or remote.

  • Practitioner certification
  • Community facilitator training
  • Train-the-trainer workshops
  • Ongoing technical mentorship

Technical Advisory

Direct access to FMNR specialists for program design, troubleshooting and context-specific adaptation across diverse agroecological zones.

  • Program design and review
  • Site assessments and feasibility
  • Gender-responsive FMNR design
  • Policy integration support

Impact Measurement

Internationally recognised methodologies for measuring FMNR outcomes — from tree density to carbon, income and food security.

  • Baseline and endline tools
  • Carbon methodology support
  • Donor-ready reporting
  • Co-published evidence briefs

Network Access

Entry into a global community of FMNR practitioners, researchers and implementing organisations across 25+ countries.

  • Global practitioner community
  • Cross-country learning exchange
  • Annual FMNR conference
  • Peer partner introductions

Communications

Storytelling, photography and case studies to help your organisation communicate FMNR's impact to donors, boards and the public.

  • Co-branded impact reports
  • Field photography and video
  • Case study development
  • Media and press support

Policy & Advocacy

Strategic support for mainstreaming FMNR in national agricultural policies, climate commitments and international frameworks.

  • Policy briefs and submissions
  • Government engagement support
  • NDC alignment guidance
  • SDG reporting linkages

How it works

From first
conversation
to first harvest.

01

Explore

We learn about your organisation, context and goals. You learn about FMNR. Together we explore whether a partnership makes sense and what form it could take.

02

Design

We co-design an implementation plan tailored to your geography, budget and existing programs — including training scope and measurement frameworks.

03

Launch

Training begins, communities are engaged and FMNR is integrated into field operations. Our technical team provides hands-on support through the critical early phase.

04

Scale

With strong roots established, we deepen and expand — reaching more communities, refining approaches and building the evidence base together.

Our partners

Trusted by world-leading organisations.

From global development agencies to local faith networks — FMNR's partner network spans every sector.

NGO — Development

World Vision

Global · 26 countries

FMNR's longest-standing institutional partner. Over 900,000 direct beneficiaries across food security and livelihoods programs in Africa, Asia and the Pacific.

Research — Agroforestry

CGIAR / ICRAF

Global · Africa focus

The World Agroforestry Centre has co-generated much of the peer-reviewed evidence base for FMNR — from soil fertility studies to tree density measurement.

Faith — Mission Agency

SIM International

Niger · West Africa

Where FMNR began. SIM's mission work in Niger gave Tony Rinaudo the platform and relationships to discover and test FMNR in some of the world's harshest conditions.

Government — Bilateral Donor

Australian DFAT

Indo-Pacific focus

Australia's Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade has funded FMNR programs as part of its overseas development assistance portfolio, including the BRACCE program in Timor-Leste.

Research — Academia

Univ. of New England

Australia

Ongoing research programs examine dryland agriculture, community NRM and the long-term ecological outcomes of FMNR adoption.

Community — Carbon

Humbo Cooperative

Ethiopia

A UN CDM-certified community cooperative managing 181,000+ tonnes of verified CO₂ sequestration — a replicable model for FMNR carbon partnerships globally.

Philanthropy — Awards

Right Livelihood Foundation

Global

Recognised Tony Rinaudo with the 2018 Right Livelihood Award, connecting the FMNR movement with a global network of change-makers and philanthropic funders.
