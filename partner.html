Why partner with FMNR

The highest return in land restoration — and the communities who do the work already have everything they need.

FMNR works across sectors because land degradation cuts across all of them. Every partner type brings something different — deep community trust, technical expertise, policy leverage, or funding — and all of them create lasting change.

We provide training, technical advisory, impact measurement tools and access to a global network of practitioners across 25+ countries. No matter your sector, there's a role for your organisation in the FMNR movement.

What partnership includes

Everything you need to make it work

Training, technical support, measurement tools and a global network — all included in every partnership.

Training & Capacity

Comprehensive programs for field staff, extension workers and community facilitators — in-country or remote.

Technical Advisory

Direct access to FMNR specialists for program design, troubleshooting and context-specific adaptation.

Impact Measurement

Internationally recognised methodologies for measuring outcomes — from tree density to carbon, income and food security.

Network Access

Entry into a global community of FMNR practitioners, researchers and implementing organisations across 25+ countries.

Communications

Storytelling, photography and case studies to communicate FMNR's impact to donors, boards and the public.

Policy & Advocacy

Strategic support for mainstreaming FMNR in national agricultural policies, climate commitments and international frameworks.

How it works

From first conversation
to first harvest

A clear, four-step pathway from initial enquiry to scaled implementation.

01

Explore

We learn about your organisation, context and goals. You learn about FMNR. Together we explore whether a partnership makes sense.

02

Design

We co-design an implementation plan tailored to your geography, budget and existing programs — including training scope and measurement.

03

Launch

Training begins, communities are engaged and FMNR is integrated into field operations. Our team provides hands-on support through the critical early phase.

04

Scale

With strong roots established, we deepen and expand — reaching more communities, refining approaches and building the evidence base together.

Our partners

Trusted by world-leading organisations

From global development agencies to local faith networks — FMNR's partner network spans every sector.

NGO — Development

World Vision

Global · 26 countries

FMNR's longest-standing institutional partner. Over 900,000 direct beneficiaries across food security and livelihoods programs.

Research — Agroforestry

CGIAR / ICRAF

Global · Africa focus

Co-generated much of the peer-reviewed evidence base for FMNR — from soil fertility studies to tree density measurement.

Faith — Mission Agency

SIM International

Niger · West Africa

Where FMNR began. SIM's mission work gave Tony Rinaudo the platform to discover and test FMNR in the world's harshest conditions.

Government — Bilateral Donor

Australian DFAT

Indo-Pacific focus

Funded FMNR programs as part of overseas development assistance, including the BRACCE program in Timor-Leste.

Research — Academia

Univ. of New England

Australia

Ongoing research on dryland agriculture, community NRM and long-term ecological outcomes of FMNR adoption.

Community — Carbon

Humbo Cooperative

Ethiopia

A UN CDM-certified community managing 181,000+ tonnes of verified CO₂ sequestration — a replicable model globally.

Philanthropy — Awards

Right Livelihood Foundation

Global

Recognised Tony Rinaudo with the 2018 Right Livelihood Award, connecting FMNR with global change-makers.
